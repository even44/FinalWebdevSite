Topical information

This page will be a two-part essay. In the first part we will talk about changes in technology, how it affects us in our daily life, and if we approve of this change. In the second part we will discuss the phrase “The person that invented the train invented the train crash”. We will give you our take on this statement, and the social responsibility of innovators.

Social changes made by innovation

In this part we will discuss how transportation has affected and changed society, more specifically the car’s role in these changes. Through the years the standard car has evolved to be faster and more efficient. You can move through great distances in a relatively short time. This means that regions and the world have gotten more interconnected compared with how the world looked 200 years ago. This has made several positive possibilities, such as having the possibility to commute between cities easily, which helps to increase the study and job opportunities of everyone. You can also meet far more people, since you aren’t locked to your geographical local area.

On the other side we can also see negative effects that the car has on society, one of these effects is accidents and security on the roads. Society now must make demands on the car manufacturers. Cars in the past were equipped with solid static chassis and steel frames. Now there is a requirement for a crumple zone that will absorb most of the energy in the event of a crash. This is to protect those inside the car instead of the car itself.

A current concern for many is the dilemma of self-driving cars. Today, car manufacturers like picture2 have much of the technology to use self-driving cars in practice, but due to ethical societal issues, much of this technology is being held back. People also argue that although the technology picture2 has is good, it’s not good enough. There are cases of crashes affected by self-driving cars.

Social responsibility of innovators

“The person that invented the train invented the train crash”. In theory is this phrase correct: without trains, there will be no train crashes. On the other side you can argue that it’s the person that first used the train wrong that invented train crashes. One can interpret the statement in many ways depending on the individual point of view. We want to further discuss this with another example. The invention of “cookies”.

Lou Montulli, the inventor of web-cookies, developed the technology to store information on different webpages. When he developed this technology in 1994, we can assume that he didn’t have any intentions that Facebook or Google would use this technology extensively to gather and sell personal information of their users. Rather, it was a new use and property of another invention that someone else invented. With that point of view, one cannot say that the inventor of trains also invented train crashes, but that it was rather a consequence of the use of the train. The inventor probably didn’t think about these consequences at all.

As a developer of a product, you should think about possible dangers or abuses that may be harmful to others or the user. Through this, the developer can reduce or eliminate the possibility of precisely these dangers. On the other hand, it is easy for a developer to overlook a possible negative side effect of the product. Often these problems are only obvious in hindsight.
